For all of us, it's VERY important to always keep the following points in mind:

  • Be cautious about emails received, even if they look like they came from someone you know.
  • Look for suspicious things: misspellings, unexpected arrival, or urgency for a response.
  • If the email is suspicious, don’t click on any links in it.
  • Examine any links to confirm the website or domain is legitimate. Also visit the website yourself, through another means, instead of clicking on a link in an email.
  • Be EXTREMELY cautious about emails with attachments – NEVER open an attachment unless you’re absolutely certain you know it’s legitimate.
  • If an attached document requires “Macros” to be enabled, this may be a sign of malware hidden in the document.
  • Call the individual who supposedly sent the email if it seems suspicious in order to verify its validity.
  • Be skeptical of emails asking for sensitive information or providing a link to which you submit sensitive information.
  • If you’re replying to an email containing sensitive information, either erase or mask the sensitive data already there.
  • Encrypt all outgoing emails that contain sensitive information.
  • If an offer in email seems too good to be true, then it probably is.
  • DELETE and FORGET any emails you believe are malicious in nature.
  • Use your work email for business-related purposes only.
